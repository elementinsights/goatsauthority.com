Celery is one of those kitchen scraps that ends up in the compost pile more often than not. If you've got goats, they'll happily put it to better use.

## Can Goats Eat Celery?

Yes, goats can eat celery without any issues. It's nontoxic, low in calories, and provides vitamins A, K, and C along with potassium.

Celery is about 95% water, making it a particularly good treat during hot summer days. It won't add much in the way of calories, but that hydration boost is a real benefit in warm weather.

Wash the celery and chop the stalks into 2 to 3 inch pieces before feeding. Those long, stringy fibers can be a mild choking concern if a goat tries to swallow a big piece whole.

## Is Celery Safe for Goats to Eat?

Completely safe. There's nothing toxic in celery, and it won't cause problems as long as you feed reasonable amounts.

Where you can run into trouble is feeding too much at once. The high water content can cause loose stool if a goat eats a whole bunch of stalks in one sitting.

Never feed your goats celery with ranch dressing, peanut butter, salt, or any other toppings. Those added ingredients can cause dehydration, bloating, or worse.

## Celery Leaves vs. Stalks

Here's something most goat owners figure out fast: goats prefer the celery leaves over the stalks. Nearly every goat I've offered leaves to has gobbled them up without hesitation.

The leaves actually contain higher concentrations of vitamins than the stalks themselves. So if your goats are picky about the crunchy part, just give them the leafy tops instead.

Both parts are equally safe. Whether your goats eat the stalks, the leaves, or the whole thing, you've got nothing to worry about.

## How Much Celery Can Goats Have?

A few chopped stalks per goat is plenty. Treats of any kind should make up no more than about 10% of your goat's daily food intake.

You can feed celery a few times per week without any problems. Mix it in with grain, toss it in a feeder, or hand-feed it as a reward during handling sessions.

Don't let celery crowd out more nutritious feeds like hay and grain. Your goats need those calorie-dense foods to maintain body condition, especially in colder months when energy demands are higher.

## Can Baby Goats Eat Celery?

Kids can eat celery once they're regularly eating solid food, usually around 2 to 3 weeks old. Before that point, they should be focused on milk and starting to explore hay.

Chop the celery into small, bite-sized pieces for kids. Those stringy fibers pose more of a choking risk for little mouths than they do for adults.

Start with just one small piece and watch how they handle it. If you notice diarrhea or bloating, hold off and try again in a few days with an even smaller amount.

## Health Benefits of Celery for Goats

The biggest benefit is hydration. On hot days, that 95% water content gives your goats an extra moisture boost on top of their regular water supply.

Vitamin A supports eye and immune health, vitamin K helps with blood clotting, and vitamin C gives the immune system a hand. The potassium supports proper muscle and nerve function.

Celery is also a low-calorie treat, which makes it a better option than sugary fruits for goats that are already carrying extra weight. You get the variety and enrichment without the calorie overload.
