Goats have a reputation for eating anything and everything. You've probably seen them chewing on tin cans, paper, and shirt sleeves. So it's a fair question to wonder if they can eat a hamburger too.

The short answer is no, goats should not eat hamburgers. Here's why.

## Can goats eat hamburgers?

Goats are herbivores. Their entire digestive system, from their teeth to their four-chamber stomach, is built to break down plant material. A hamburger is beef, and beef is not something a goat's body knows what to do with.

Will a goat die from grabbing a bite of your burger at a cookout? Probably not. But meat is not food for goats, and feeding it to them on purpose is a bad idea. Their rumen is full of bacteria that specialize in fermenting cellulose from hay, leaves, and browse. Those bacteria have no use for animal protein and fat.

## Why goats are herbivores, not omnivores

A goat's rumen works like a big fermentation vat. Plant fiber goes in, billions of microbes break it down, and the goat absorbs the nutrients. This system has evolved over thousands of years to handle grasses, weeds, bark, and leaves.

Meat doesn't ferment the same way plant fiber does. When a goat eats meat, it sits in the rumen and can cause digestive upset, gas, and bloating. The high fat content in hamburger meat is especially hard on a ruminant's system, which is [designed to digest plant matter](/can-goats-digest-anything/). Their bodies simply aren't wired for it.

## What happens if a goat eats meat?

If your goat swiped a piece of hamburger off someone's plate, don't panic. A one-time nibble of a small amount is unlikely to cause serious harm. You might notice looser stools for a day or two as the rumen works through something it wasn't designed to process.

Watch for signs of bloat or lethargy over the next 24 hours. If the goat is eating hay, drinking water, and acting like its usual self, it'll be fine. Offer some [hay cubes](/can-goats-eat-hay-cubes/) to help settle the rumen with extra fiber. Just don't let it become a habit. Repeated exposure to meat products can throw off the balance of microbes in the rumen and cause chronic digestive issues.

## Why goats nibble on strange things

People assume [goats eat everything](/can-goats-eat-everything/) because they see them mouthing random objects. The truth is goats are browsers, not grazers. They explore their world with their lips and tongue, which means they'll taste-test just about anything.

When a goat chews on a tin can, it's after the paper label and the glue, not the metal. You might even catch them nibbling on [kitchen leftovers](/can-i-feed-my-goat-leftovers/) if they get the chance. When it mouths your jacket, it's curious about the salt from your sweat. This investigative behavior doesn't mean they need or want meat in their diet. They're just nosy animals that check everything out with their mouths.

## What to actually feed your goats

Good [grass hay](/what-kind-of-hay-do-goats-eat/) should make up the bulk of a goat's diet. Browse, which includes leaves, twigs, bark, and weeds, is what goats naturally prefer and what keeps their rumen healthiest.

Grain like [oats](/can-goats-eat-oats/) can be fed in moderation to goats that need extra calories, like pregnant or lactating does. A quality loose mineral supplement formulated for goats fills in any nutritional gaps. Fresh water available at all times rounds out what a goat needs to stay healthy. That's the whole list, and hamburgers aren't on it.

## Final Thoughts

Goats are incredible animals, but they're plant eaters through and through. A hamburger offers nothing a goat's digestive system can use, and the fat, salt, and preservatives can cause problems.

If your goat managed to steal a bite of meat, it'll almost certainly be fine. But feeding hamburgers to goats on purpose is something no experienced goat keeper would recommend. Stick with hay, browse, and proper goat feed, and leave the burgers for yourself.
