## Can goats eat kiwi leaves?

[Goats are known for being able to eat just about anything](/can-goats-eat-everything/), but that doesn't mean that everything is good for them. Kiwi leaves, for example, can be harmful to goats if they consume too much of them.

The leaves contain a compound called lactucarium, which can cause digestive and nervous system problems in goats.

Lactucarium is also known to be a mild sedative, so it's possible that goats who consume too many kiwi leaves may become drowsy or even fall asleep.

For these reasons, it's best to avoid feeding kiwi leaves to goats. If you have goats on your property, make sure to keep them away from any kiwi plants.

## Can goats eat kiwi stems?

Goats are curious creatures and will often nibble on things that they shouldn't eat. But, kiwi stems are not toxic to goats and can actually be quite nutritious.

Goats usually prefer to [eat the leaves of plants](/can-goats-eat-any-plant/) rather than the stems, but they will happily munch on kiwi stems if they are available.

Kiwi stems are a good source of fiber and also contain minerals such as magnesium and calcium. Another thing to note is kiwi stems contain compounds that can help to reduce inflammation in the digestive system.

While there's no need to feed goats specifically kiwi stems, they can be a healthy part of their diet if they are available.

## Can goats eat kiwi seeds?

Kiwi seeds are edible to goats. In actuality, numerous animals can digest the tiny, tough seeds of fruits like kiwis.

Despite this, you must keep in mind that goats can choke on seeds, so only give them a small amount at a time.

Additionally, the seeds should be chewed or crushed before consumption to assist prevent them from getting stuck in the goat's throat. Kiwi seeds are generally a nutritious treat for goats, but they should only be given occasionally.

## Can a lactating goat eat kiwi?

Kiwis are an excellent source of vitamins and minerals, including vitamin C, potassium, and folate. They are also relatively low in sugar compared to other fruits.

### How to Prepare Kiwi for Your Goats

The simplest way to serve kiwi is to slice it in half and let your goats scoop out the flesh with their tongues. Most goats will eat the skin too, which is actually where much of the fiber is concentrated.

For smaller goats or kids that you are introducing to kiwi for the first time, mash the flesh and mix it into their grain. This reduces the choking risk and lets them get used to the flavor without having to deal with the fuzzy skin.

Remove any stickers or labels from store-bought kiwi before offering it. Goats will eat the sticker without hesitation, and the adhesive is not something you want in their digestive system.

### Growing Kiwi Vines for Your Goat Herd

If you have the right climate, growing your own kiwi vines can provide a renewable treat source for your goats. Hardy kiwi varieties (Actinidia arguta) can survive winters as cold as USDA Zone 4 and produce smaller, smooth-skinned fruit that goats find easier to eat.

Plant the vines along a sturdy fence or trellis that your goats cannot reach. Kiwi vines need a few years before they start producing fruit, and goats will strip the leaves and bark off young plants if they get access.

Once the vines are mature and producing, you can harvest the fruit and offer it as a treat. A single productive kiwi vine can yield 50 to 100 pounds of fruit per season, which is more than enough for a small herd.
